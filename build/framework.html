Our Framework

An evolving pedagogy of modern skills emerging from the Information Age

What is Spark?

Spark is a student driven research initiative that is looking to arm teacherswith the knowledge and tools to teach high school students. The inspiration from this project came from our personal experience and the difficulty in finding a major in college. We all found our current major Informatics eventually, but the time it took was much too long.

We took a survey within the major inquiring whether or not people would have liked to have been introduced to Informatics or similar topics earlier. The answers was almost uninimously yes and that the ideal time would have been during high school.

We examined high curriculums of nearby high schools and recognized that they are devoid of classes that teach technical computer literacy and design thinking.

The Problem

Students, who would possibly be interested in informatics (dormant interest), are not getting introduced to informatics related topics (UX/UI, web dev, IA, etc.) early enough in life.

Our Goal

Introduce students to informatics related topics earlier in life (e.g. high school) to help them spark a dormant interest in the topics.

The Solution

  1. Develop a framework of curriculum, best practices, and findings that allow organizations or teachers to effectively bring informatics to high schools.
  2. Stipulate and implement a specific pilot study that will inform our framework.

Our Audiences

  1. Our framework content is targeted toward high school students who have not been introduced or who have minimal introduction to the topics of informatics
  2. Our framework itself is targeting
    • organizations with an interest in teaching informatics topics
    • existing teachers in formal education systems with in interest in teaching informatics topics
    • people seeking to share their knowledge and skills in information science with younger than college audiences
